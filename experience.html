The Kedaireka Merdeka Campus Platform (hereinafter referred to as "Kedaireka") is a platform provided, managed, and developed by the Directorate General of Higher Education, Research, and Technology and the Directorate General of Vocational Education of the Ministry of Education, Culture, Research, and Technology of the Republic of Indonesia (hereinafter referred to as the "Ministry"). Kedaireka is an acronym for Collaboration between the Business World and Creative Creations. Kedaireka can also be interpreted as Indonesia's Sovereignty in Creativity. The Ministry aims to establish a platform for collaboration between higher education institutions and the Business World and Industry, as well as related parties. The Kedaireka Merdeka Campus Platform is expected to serve as a center for meetings and communication between creative creators in Higher Education (hereinafter referred to as "Dikti Insiders") and the Business World and Industry.

Technical Expert Team : Lab Test of Solar Street Light, Lamongan (2022)

Specification

Information

Time Period : Juni 2022 - Juni 2023
Job Position : As a Team of Technical Experts

Description

This project is a collaboration between the Lamongan District Prosecutor's Office and my team (UNAIR). The Lamongan District Prosecutor's Office commissioned the services of my team to conduct analysis and laboratory testing of solar-powered street lights for the purpose of an investigation into a criminal case. The team, consisting of 10 members, conducted inspections and analyses of 1600 units of solar-powered street lights, as well as conducted laboratory tests on the electrical components of the lights. This was done to ensure the conformity of the components with the specified specifications. The project spanned one year from the beginning until the case was closed. The stages involved encompassed checking all units, conducting laboratory testing, and reporting the test results.

Description

Klumutan's Guidance is a digital village map application that was created during my Community Service Program (KKN) in Klumutan Village, Saradan, Madiun. This application was developed in response to the concerns expressed by the Klumutan community. Klumutan Village is a relatively large village, divided into 6 distinct and separate hamlets. For example, if someone from hamlet A is visiting hamlet B and encounters issues like a flat tire, an accident, or needs information about important locations within the hamlet or their current location, this application addresses these challenges. The way this application functions is by providing directions to the required destination. The locations available in the application are added by the previous host, while users can only utilize the services provided within the application.
Why not use Google Maps? Haha, I'm sure many will ask that question. Well, the answer is that during discussions with several village officials, they requested that the added locations should not be accessible to outsiders. This limitation ensures that the application's scope remains local to Klumutan Village only, in consideration of the village's privacy concerns. Given these issues and requirements, I developed the Klumutan Guidance application within a span of 5 days. I began by integrating the Google Developer Console API key into the Android Studio project, then proceeded to create functions for current location, adding markers, destination locations, obtaining directions, uploading photos, and incorporating features that were deemed necessary for the application. I opted for Realtime Database as the storage for latitude and longitude data of the markers on the maps. Meanwhile, photo data is stored in Firebase Cloud Storage. The challenges I encountered during the development of this application primarily revolved around creating a framework and functional logic that had to be clear and precise. This was crucial for the application's proper functioning
As a result, this application has been launched and handed over to the village authorities for use and further development on July 31, 2023, at the Klumutan Village Hall, Saradan, Madiun.
